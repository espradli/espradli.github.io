🗼 Tokyo — Apr 25 to Apr 30 (6 nights)

Arrive Narita (NRT) at 4:00 PM + 5 full days to explore

🗺️ Activities
Arrival Evening — Apr 25

Land at 4 PM, get to hotel, grab dinner in the neighborhood. Low-key first night.

Must-Do (5 full days — pick your days)
  • Shibuya + Harajuku + Meiji Shrine — Walking loop: Shibuya Crossing → Takeshita Street → Meiji Shrine → Yoyogi Park. Half day
  • Asakusa (Senso-ji) — Temple, Nakamise shopping street, Sumida River walk with Skytree backdrop. Half day
  • Shinjuku evening — Golden Gai bar district, Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane). Best at night — pair with dinner
  • Tsukiji Outer Market — Morning street food and fresh seafood breakfast. Pair with an afternoon activity
  • TeamLab Borderless or Planets — Pick one. Book timed tickets in advance
🏛️ Design & Architecture
  • 21_21 DESIGN SIGHT — Tadao Ando-designed design museum in Tokyo Midtown (80% underground). Rotating exhibitions on design in everyday life. Co-founded by Issey Miyake. Half day
  • MoN Takanawa: Museum of Narratives — Brand new (opened Mar 28, 2026). Immersive cultural complex at Takanawa Gateway Station blending tradition and tech. Biannual themes — opening theme is "Life as Culture." Half day
  • Mori Art Museum — Contemporary art on the 53rd floor of Roppongi Hills. Rotating major exhibitions + city views from the observation deck
  • National Museum of Modern Art (MOMAT) — Near the Imperial Palace. Japanese modern art from 1900s onward. Current exhibition runs through May 10
🚃 Day Trips (pick one)
  • Kamakura — Great Buddha, coastal temples, laid-back beach town (~1hr by train). Full day
  • Nikko — Ornate shrines and temples in the mountains, UNESCO World Heritage (~2hr by train). Full day
If Time/Energy Allows
  • Akihabara — Arcades, electronics, anime. Easy to drop in for a couple hours
  • Shinjuku Gyoen — Beautiful garden, great for a chill afternoon
🎌 Golden Week Events (Apr 29–30)
  • Meiji Shrine Spring Grand Festival — Ancient court dances (bugaku), Noh theater, Kyogen comedy, and traditional biwa lute performances
  • Senso-ji special events — Golden Week ceremonies and extra market stalls in Asakusa
  • Koinobori (carp streamers) — Tokyo Tower displays 333 colorful carp streamers; visible across parks and neighborhoods
  • Shōwa Day (Apr 29) — Parks and gardens offer free or discounted admission; Shinjuku Gyoen is especially popular

Crowds increase significantly from Apr 29 onward. Popular spots will be packed — embrace it or plan for early mornings.

🛍️ Shopping

Japan-made brands are significantly cheaper here — 20-40% less than US prices, plus 10% tax-free for tourists (show passport, min ¥5,000 per store).

Brands Worth Buying in Japan
  • Onitsuka Tiger — 20-30% cheaper than US. Flagship stores in Omotesando (3 shops including NIPPON MADE for Japan-exclusive models) and Shibuya. Japan-only colorways and limited editions you can't get stateside
  • Grand Seiko — 15-25% cheaper after tax-free. Grand Seiko Boutique Ginza is the flagship. Also available at Bic Camera, Yodobashi, and authorized dealers. Japan-exclusive (JDM) models only sold domestically. RESEARCH MODELS
  • Montbell — 30-50% cheaper than US prices. Tokyo flagship in Shibuya (huge multi-floor store). Japan line has lighter fabrics and models not sold in the US
  • Uniqlo / MUJI — Japan-exclusive lines and collabs not available overseas. Uniqlo Ginza flagship has Japan-only items. MUJI Ginza is the world's largest. Both significantly cheaper than US
  • Japanese whisky — Hibiki, Yamazaki, Hakushu all cheaper at source. Bic Camera is surprisingly great — tax-free + store discounts stack. Also check Don Quijote and department store basements
  • Japanese knives — Kappabashi Street (Kitchen Town). Handmade knives at a fraction of import prices
  • Japanese denim — Okayama brands at Hinoya (Shibuya). Also check Kapital in Ebisu
Streetwear & Fashion
  • Dover Street Market Ginza — Rei Kawakubo's concept store. Part gallery, part shop. Comme des Garçons, NEIGHBORHOOD collabs, rotating Japan-exclusive drops. A must if you're into design
  • BEAMS Japan — Shinjuku, 6 floors. Curated Japanese fashion, crafts, food, art. Constant Japan-exclusive collabs
  • Human Made — Nigo's brand (BAPE founder). Harajuku flagship. 20-30% less than overseas resale
  • Kapital — Cult Japanese denim/Americana. Ebisu flagship. Barely available outside Japan, heavily marked up when it is
  • Visvim — High-end Japanese heritage. Tokyo stores carry pieces not sold anywhere else
Everyday Deals
  • Drugstores (Matsumoto Kiyoshi, Don Quijote) — Japanese sunscreen, sheet masks, eye drops (Rohto), skincare (Shiseido, SK-II) at a fraction of US import prices. The real value is here, not department store counters
  • Don Quijote (Donki) — Chaotic discount megastore. Tax-free, open late/24hr. Everything from snacks to electronics to luxury goods. Shibuya Mega Donki is an experience in itself

💡 Tax-free: Show your passport at checkout for purchases over ¥5,000. The 10% consumption tax is removed at point of sale. Your trip is before the Nov 2026 rule change, so you still get the instant discount.

Golden Week festivities — explore on foot and by metro

🗺️ Activities
May 1 — Arrival Evening

Arrive from Tokyo via Shinkansen. Settle in, then hit Dōtonbori for an evening street food crawl.

Fushimi Inari, Kinkaku-ji, Arashiyama bamboo grove (~30 min by train). Go early — Fushimi Inari by 7-8 AM to beat Golden Week crowds, then work north through the city.

May 3 — Nara + Osaka (Constitution Day)

Morning half-day in Nara — deer park, Todai-ji Temple (~45 min by train). Back to Osaka by early afternoon for:

  • Osaka Castle — Golden Week illuminations and cultural performances
  • Shinsekai — Retro neighborhood, kushikatsu, Tsutenkaku Tower
  • Kuromon Market — "Osaka's Kitchen," fresh seafood stalls
🏛️ Design & Architecture
  • National Museum of Art, Osaka (NMAO) — Entirely underground museum on Nakanoshima island, designed by César Pelli. Striking bamboo-inspired steel sculpture above ground. Contemporary art from 1945 onward. Easy to pair with Nakanoshima area exploring
  • Nakanoshima Museum of Art — Opened 2022, bold black cube exterior. 6,000+ works of modern art and design from the 19th century onward. Right next to NMAO — do both in one visit
If Time/Energy Allows
  • Sumiyoshi Taisha — One of Japan's oldest shrines, beautiful arched bridge
  • Umeda Sky Building — Floating garden observatory, sunset views
🎌 Golden Week Events (May 1–3)
  • Dōtonbori street food explosion — Extra vendors, special limited-edition festival foods, electric atmosphere
  • Osaka Castle special events — Extended hours, illuminations, and cultural performances
  • Shitennoji Temple Walpurgis Market — Large flea market and special ceremonies
  • Kyoto Golden Week — Temples hold special ceremonies; Kamo Kurabeuma (sacred horse race) at Kamigamo Shrine

Crowds increase significantly. May 3 (Constitution Day) is one of the busiest days of the year — plan for early mornings.

🏔️ Option A: Takayama (高山) — PREFERRED

Mountain town in the Japanese Alps — Edo-period old town. Genuinely rural and countryside. Small mountain town (~90k population) with preserved wooden merchant houses, narrow streets, riverside morning markets. Very laid-back pace.

From Osaka: ~3hr by car · ~4hr by train (via Nagoya)
To Tokyo: ~3.5-4hr by car · ~4hr by train (via Nagoya shinkansen)

🗺️ Activities & Day Trips
In Town (daily)
  • Old Town (Sanmachi Suji) — Preserved Edo-period merchant streets, sake breweries with tastings, craft shops
  • Miyagawa Morning Market — Daily along the river, local produce and snacks
Must-Do Day Trips (pick 2-3)
  • Shirakawa-go — UNESCO thatched-roof village (~50 min by car / bus). Full day
  • Kamikochi — Alpine valley, stunning hiking (~1.5 hr, shuttle bus required from Hirayu). Full day
  • Okuhida Onsen — Hot spring villages with mountain views (~1 hr by car / bus). Half day or overnight
If Time/Energy Allows
  • Hida Furukawa — Charming town with koi-filled canals (~15 min by car / train). Half day
  • Shinhotaka Ropeway — Double-decker cable car, panoramic Alps views (~1.5 hr by car / bus). Half day
  • Gujo Hachiman — Castle town with crystal-clear rivers (~1.5 hr by car · ⚠️ car strongly recommended). Full day

Most day trips are doable by bus from Takayama Bus Center (Nohi Bus). A car adds flexibility but isn't essential except for Gujo Hachiman.

🎌 Golden Week Events (May 4–6)
  • Koinobori over the Miyagawa River — Hundreds of colorful carp streamers strung across the river through the old town
  • Children's Day (May 5) — Families display samurai helmets (kabuto) and dolls; local shops sell kashiwamochi and chimaki sweets
  • Greenery Day nature events (May 4) — Special guided walks and nature appreciation events in the surrounding Japanese Alps
  • Shirakawa-go Golden Week — The UNESCO village is especially festive with spring decorations, though expect larger crowds
🍣 Food & Dining
  • Hida beef sushi — Takayama's signature: wagyu nigiri served on the street and in restaurants. Kyoshi and Sakaguchiya are popular walk-up spots. No reservation needed
  • Hida beef fine dining — A5-grade wagyu sukiyaki, shabu-shabu, and steak. Try Matsuki-ushi (A5 only, char-grilled) or Suzuya. RESERVE
  • Takayama ramen — Distinctive curly noodles in soy-based broth, unique to the region
  • Sansai cuisine — Foraged mountain vegetables, hyper-local and seasonal
  • Miyagawa Morning Market — Daily along the river, local produce and snacks
  • Sake breweries — 6-7 in the old town, many with tastings
  • Kaiseki ryokan dining — Multi-course traditional meals at local inns. Consider booking at least one ryokan dinner even if staying at an Airbnb — many ryokans accept dinner-only guests. RESERVE
  • 🎌 Sake brewery Golden Week specials — Several breweries offer limited tastings and special seasonal releases
  • 🎌 Extended morning market — Miyagawa Morning Market has extra vendors and special Golden Week offerings

⚠️ No Michelin-starred restaurants in Takayama. The food scene is exceptional but focused on local specialties and casual/ryokan dining. For high-end omakase, save that for Tokyo and Osaka.

🏔️ Option B: Kanazawa (金沢)

Coastal city — "Little Kyoto" with world-class seafood. Mid-size city (~460k) that was the wealthiest domain in Edo-period Japan. Beautifully preserved but more urban than Takayama. Fewer tourists than Kyoto.

From Osaka: ~2.5hr by car · ~2.5hr by JR Thunderbird (direct)
To Tokyo: ~2.5hr by car · ~2.5hr by Hokuriku Shinkansen (direct)

🗺️ Activities & Day Trips
In Town (daily)
  • Kenroku-en Garden — One of Japan's top three gardens. Pair with Kanazawa Castle next door
  • 21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art — Iconic circular glass building by SANAA (Sejima + Nishizawa). Free public zone with outdoor installations; ticketed galleries with works by Leandro Erlich ("Swimming Pool"), James Turrell, and rotating contemporary exhibits. The architecture alone is worth the visit
  • Higashi Chaya District — Historic geisha quarter, tea houses, gold leaf shops
  • Omi-cho Market — "Kanazawa's Kitchen," 200+ stalls, walk-up sushi counters
  • D.T. Suzuki Museum — Minimalist museum by architect Yoshio Taniguchi (who designed MoMA NYC's expansion). Contemplative water garden and meditation spaces
Must-Do Day Trips (pick 2-3)
  • Shirakawa-go — UNESCO thatched-roof village (~1.5 hr by car / Nohi Bus). Full day
  • Kaga Onsen — Four hot spring towns (~30 min by car / train). Half day or overnight
  • Noto Peninsula — Rugged coastline, fishing villages (~1.5 hr by car · ⚠️ car strongly recommended). Full day
If Time/Energy Allows
  • Fukui / Eiheiji Temple — Major Zen Buddhist temple (~1 hr by car / JR + bus). Half day
  • Hakusan National Park — Mountain hiking, alpine meadows (~1.5 hr by car · ⚠️ car recommended). Full day

Kanazawa city itself is very walkable + has good buses. A car is most useful for Noto Peninsula and Hakusan.

🎌 Golden Week Events (May 4–6)
  • Kenroku-en Garden spring illuminations — Special evening light-up events showcasing fresh greenery and spring flowers
  • Kanazawa Castle light-up — Castle grounds illuminated for Golden Week evenings
  • Children's Day (May 5) — Koinobori displays throughout the city; special family events at parks and shrines
  • Higashi Chaya District — The geisha district hosts special tea ceremony events and cultural demonstrations
🍣 Food & Dining
  • Michelin sushi (2⭐) — Otome Sushi and Mekumi are both 2-star sushi restaurants with incredible Sea of Japan fish. ¥15,000–30,000. RESERVE EARLY
  • Michelin sushi (1⭐) — Kikuya, Sushi Kibatani, Sushi Okauma, Sushiya Kozakura — multiple 1-star sushi options. RESERVE
  • Ryori Komatsu (3⭐) — Kanazawa's only 3-star restaurant. Exceptional kaiseki. ¥30,000+. RESERVE EARLY
  • Zeniya (2⭐) — Legendary kaiseki, famous butter-grilled abalone steak. RESERVE EARLY
  • Koizumi (2⭐) — Michelin 2-star tempura. RESERVE
  • Omi-cho Market — "Kanazawa's Kitchen," 200+ stalls, incredible fresh seafood. Walk-up sushi counters, no reservation needed
  • Snow crab, sweet shrimp, yellowtail — Some of the best seafood in Japan
  • Kanazawa-style curry — Thick dark curry on a steel plate, local comfort food
  • Gold leaf everything — 99% of Japan's gold leaf. Gold leaf ice cream, sweets, beer
  • Kaga cuisine — Refined multi-course dining from the Maeda clan era
  • 🎌 Omi-cho Market Golden Week — Extended hours and special seasonal seafood offerings

⚠️ Kanazawa has 1 three-star, 10+ two-star, and 15+ one-star Michelin restaurants — a world-class dining city. Top spots book out weeks in advance.
